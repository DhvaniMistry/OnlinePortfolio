Beyond Code

There’s more to me than terminals and test suites. This page is a little snapshot of what keeps me grounded and excited outside of pure engineering: lifting, plants, community work, good food, travel, and a very cute dog.

Strength Training & Intramural Sports

The gym and intramural volleyball are where I practice consistency, discipline, and teamwork—the same habits I lean on when debugging stubborn issues or working in an engineering team.

I like setting realistic goals, tracking progress, and celebrating small wins— whether that's adding a bit more weight, playing a tighter game with my team, or finally landing a tricky feature without regressions.

  • Goal-oriented training with a focus on long-term progress.
  • Intramural volleyball has taught me how to communicate quickly and trust my team.
  • Balancing classes, internships, TA work, and training forces me to manage time intentionally.

Plant Corner

My plants are tiny systems I get to care for. I experiment with light, watering, and repotting the same way I experiment with metrics, logs, and configuration in code.

Keeping plants alive (and happy) keeps me observant. If something looks off, I try to hypothesize, adjust one variable, and wait to see results—a nice mirror of debugging and performance tuning.

Indoor plant collection, propagation experiments, and a growing wish list.
Learning how different plants respond to watering schedules and soil mixes.
Observation → hypothesis → small changes → results. Same loop as debugging.
